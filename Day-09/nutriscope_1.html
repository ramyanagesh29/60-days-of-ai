Methodology

Nutrition Sources & References

Food composition values are approximated from public references including USDA FoodData Central and the Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT 2017, National Institute of Nutrition, ICMR) — figures are per 100g/100ml edible portion and rounded for readability.
Daily targets use the Mifflin–St Jeor equation for resting energy needs, scaled by activity factor, with macro splits (20% protein / 55% carbohydrate / 25% fat) informed by general ICMR-NIN and Dietary Guidelines for Americans ranges.
Micronutrient RDAs (iron, calcium, vitamin C, vitamin D, B12, zinc, magnesium, potassium, vitamin A, folate) reflect commonly cited adult reference intakes and will differ from a personalized clinical assessment.
Cooking method, ripeness, brand, and portion estimation all shift real values — treat every number here as directional, not exact.
NutriScope runs entirely in your browser — no data leaves this tab, nothing is saved after refresh.
This is an educational estimation tool, not medical or dietetic advice. See Sources above for methodology.
